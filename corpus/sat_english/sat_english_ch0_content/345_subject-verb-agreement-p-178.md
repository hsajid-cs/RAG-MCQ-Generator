1. Appear (pl.): the subject is the plural noun galaxies, which is separated from the verb by the non-essential clause far from being randomly scattered throughout the universe.
2. Have (pl.): the subject is the compound noun (plural) The expansion of roads and the construction of a chemical plant.
3. Explore (pl.): the subject is the plural noun works, which is separated from the verb by the prepositional phrase of Chippewa author Louise Erdrich.
4. Is (sing., correct): the subject is any, which is singular (short for any one), which takes a singular verb.
5. Seems (sing.): the subject is the singular pronoun each (short for each one), which is separated from the verb by the prepositional phrase of the compositions by jazz musician Thelonius Monk.

9. Was (sing., correct): the subject is the singular noun (technically a gerund) founding, which is separated from the verb by the prepositional phrase by friends James Kelly, John Wheeler, and Joseph Forrest.
10. Was (sing.): the subject is the singular pronoun neither, which is short for neither one.

12.2 Makes (sing., correct): the subject is having, which as a gerund must be singular.
12.3 Changes (sing., correct): the subject is what, which is always singular. Don't be fooled by the plural noun tools, which appears closer to the verb. Although this word is part of the complete subject (What constitutes the right tools), it does not affect the number of the verb.
