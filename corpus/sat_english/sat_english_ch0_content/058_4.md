# Sentences and Fragments

Before you read any further, try the exercise below. When you look at its title, you might roll your eyes and think, "Well duh, of course I know what a sentence is," but sometimes it isn't nearly as obvious as you might assume. The ability to distinguish between sentences and fragments is the basis for correctly using the punctuation discussed in the following chapters. If you cannot tell when a statement is and is not a sentence, you will find it extremely difficult to know when to use periods, commas, semicolons, and colons.
