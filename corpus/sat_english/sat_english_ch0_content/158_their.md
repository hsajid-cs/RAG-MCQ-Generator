## Their

Incorrect: Deactivated viruses form the basis of many vaccines known for they're effectiveness in preventing disease.

Incorrect: Deactivated viruses form the basis of many vaccines known for there effectiveness in preventing disease.

Correct: Deactivated viruses form the basis of many vaccines known for their effectiveness in preventing disease.

Because you would not say, Deactivated viruses form the basis of many vaccines known for they are effectiveness, no apostrophe is needed. The sentence does not refer to a place, so there is not correct either.
