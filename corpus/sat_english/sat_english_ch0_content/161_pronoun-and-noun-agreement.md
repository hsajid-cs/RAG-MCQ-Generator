## Pronoun and Noun Agreement

Many questions that test apostrophes also test pronoun agreement simultaneously. As a result, it is impossible to discuss one without discussing the other.

A pronoun must agree with the noun to which it refers, i.e., its antecedent or referent. Singular pronouns (e.g., she, it) must agree with singular nouns, and plural pronouns (e.g., they) must agree with plural nouns.
